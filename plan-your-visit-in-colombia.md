# 🌎 Plan Your Visit to Colombia

Colombia is a diverse and vibrant destination with mountains, beaches, rainforests, and historic cities. Planning ahead helps you enjoy a safe, smooth, and memorable trip.

## Best Time to Visit
Colombia has no traditional seasons due to its equatorial location. Weather varies by region:
- **Bogotá:** Cool year‑round; driest months are December–March and July–August.
- **Medellín:** Spring‑like climate all year; mild temperatures.
- **Cartagena & Caribbean Coast:** Hot and humid; best months are December–April.
- **Coffee Region:** Warm days, cool nights; occasional showers year‑round.

## Flights to Colombia
Major international airports include:
- **Bogotá (BOG)** — Main hub for long‑haul flights.
- **Medellín (MDE)** — Popular for regional and international routes.
- **Cartagena (CTG)** — Gateway for Caribbean travelers.
- **Cali (CLO)** — Southern Colombia access point.

Direct flights commonly arrive from:
- The United States (Miami, New York, Houston, Atlanta)
- Canada (Toronto, Montreal)
- Europe (Madrid, Barcelona, Amsterdam)
- Latin America (Mexico City, Lima, Santiago)

## Money and Payments
- **Currency:** Colombian Peso (COP).
- **ATMs:** Widely available in cities and airports.
- **Cards:** Credit/debit cards accepted in most urban areas.
- **Tipping:** Common but not mandatory; restaurants often include a 10% service charge.

## Getting Around Colombia
- **Domestic flights:** Fastest way to travel between major cities.
- **Intercity buses:** Affordable and comfortable for long distances.
- **Taxis & ride‑hailing apps:** Uber, Didi, Cabify, and InDriver operate in major cities.
- **Car rentals:** Available but not recommended for first‑time visitors due to traffic and road conditions.

## Staying Safe
Colombia is generally safe for tourists when following basic precautions:
- Stay in well‑reviewed neighborhoods and accommodations.
- Use registered taxis or ride‑hailing apps.
- Avoid displaying valuables in crowded areas.
- Be cautious at night, especially in unfamiliar neighborhoods.
- Keep digital copies of your passport and travel documents.
- Follow local advice regarding areas to avoid.

## Popular Activities
- Explore Cartagena’s walled city.
- Visit Medellín’s Comuna 13 and cable cars.
- Tour coffee farms in the Coffee Triangle.
- Hike Cocora Valley’s wax palm forests.
- Relax on Caribbean beaches like San Andrés or Tayrona.
- Discover Bogotá’s museums and historic La Candelaria.

## Emergency Phone Numbers
- **Police:** 123  
- **Ambulance:** 123  
- **Fire:** 119

## Important Reminder
Foreign visitors must complete the mandatory [online Check‑MIG form](https://checkmigcolombia.us/) before traveling to Colombia. This online form is required for smooth entry into the country.
